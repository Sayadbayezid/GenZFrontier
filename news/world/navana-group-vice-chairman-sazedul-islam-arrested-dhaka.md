# Navana Group Vice Chairman Sazedul Islam Arrested in Dhaka Over Labour Law Case

**Business & Law Desk | GenZ Frontier | June 26, 2026**

In a significant development within the corporate and legal sectors, **Navana Group Vice Chairman Sazedul Islam** was arrested from his residence in the capital's Banani area early Wednesday morning. 

The arrest was executed in connection with an outstanding warrant issued against him by a labour court, involving alleged violations of labour laws.

---

## The Early Morning Arrest at Banani

The operation was conducted in the early hours of Wednesday. According to law enforcement sources, a police team arrived at his residence in Banani to carry out the arrest based on a court-issued warrant. 

**Mahbubur Rahman**, the Officer-in-Charge (OC) of Tejgaon Industrial Area Police Station, officially confirmed the incident to the media, stating that the police were executing a legal order directed by the court. 

> "There was a specific arrest warrant against Sazedul Islam in a labour court case. Acting on that warrant, he was arrested at around 7:00 AM on Wednesday," OC Mahbubur Rahman informed reporters.

## Background of the Labour Court Case

While the initial arrest warrant executed by the police did not contain exhaustive details of the specific allegations at the time of the arrest, case-related records indicate that the lawsuit stems from a dispute over the alleged non-payment of financial dues. 

The case was reportedly filed by a former senior executive of the company, who alleged that a significant amount of legitimate financial dues remained unpaid following his departure from the organization. The labour court had previously issued summons, and the arrest warrant was subsequently issued to ensure compliance with the legal proceedings.

## Police Action and Court Presentation

Following the successful execution of the arrest warrant at his Banani residence, Sazedul Islam was immediately transported to the Tejgaon Industrial Area Police Station for routine processing.

The police did not detain him at the station for long, as standard procedure requires the accused to be presented before a judicial magistrate. 

"Following his arrest, Sazedul Islam was taken to the police station and later sent directly to the court," OC Mahbubur Rahman added. Later in the day, the accused was produced before the Dhaka Labour Court, where his legal counsel proceeded to navigate the bail procedures.
