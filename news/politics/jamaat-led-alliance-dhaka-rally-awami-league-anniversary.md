# Jamaat-Led 11-Party Alliance Calls Massive Dhaka Rally on Awami League Anniversary to Demand Justice

**GenZ Frontier Political Desk | June 22, 2026**

The Jamaat-e-Islami-led 11-party political alliance has announced a massive protest rally in Dhaka scheduled for tomorrow, **June 23**. The date strategically coincides with the founding anniversary of the Awami League—the former ruling party whose political activities currently remain banned in Bangladesh following the historic mass uprising of 2024. 

The primary objective of the rally is to demand rapid trials and strict accountability for all "enforced disappearances, killings, and massacres committed by the fascist Awami League" during its nearly two-decade grip on power. 

---

## The Dhaka Rally: Time, Venue, and Key Speakers

The central rally will take place at **Bijoynagar at 5:00 PM** tomorrow. Jamaat-e-Islami Ameer and the current Opposition Leader, **Shafiqur Rahman**, is scheduled to attend and address the massive gathering as the chief guest. 

The comprehensive political programs were officially announced during a press briefing held at Jamaat-e-Islami's central office in Dhaka's Moghbazar on Monday morning. Beyond historical grievances, the alliance outlined several pressing contemporary issues driving their current political movement:
*   **Economic Strain:** Protesting the unchecked rise in the prices of essential commodities affecting daily life.
*   **Law and Order:** Condemning the recent deterioration of national security and public safety.
*   **Political Repression:** Standing against the continued repression and harassment of opposition political factions.
*   **National Sovereignty:** Protesting alleged domestic and foreign conspiracies actively working against the country's interests.

---

## June 23: "A Painful Day in Political History"

June 23 has traditionally been celebrated by Awami League loyalists as the party's founding anniversary. However, the 11-party bloc is utilizing the day to present a stark counter-narrative. 

Speaking at the briefing, **Hamidur Rahman Azad**, Jamaat Assistant Secretary General and the liaison committee coordinator for the alliance, explicitly described June 23 as a "painful day" in Bangladesh's political history. 

Azad alleged that over the previous 17 years, the nation's independence, sovereignty, and the fundamental voting rights of its citizens had been systematically compromised merely to keep the former autocratic government in power. 

> "Attempts are actively underway to rehabilitate fascism despite the sacrifices of the 2024 mass uprising. We must remain vigilant," Azad warned the press.

## A Demand for Stalled Justice: From Pilkhana to the July Uprising

A major focal point of the press briefing was the pace of ongoing judicial processes. Azad expressed deep frustration that justice remains elusive over a long series of dark chapters in the nation's history. 

He specifically highlighted:
*   The **2009 Pilkhana tragedy**, which saw the massacre of top military officers.
*   Thousands of documented cases of **enforced disappearances** and **extrajudicial killings** orchestrated by state mechanisms.
*   The **July-August 2024 mass killings**, where hundreds of students and citizens were gunned down during the anti-discrimination movement.

Azad noted a concerning shift in governmental momentum. According to him, while the post-revolution *interim government* had significantly accelerated the judicial process and tribunals to address these crimes, the pace of accountability has noticeably slowed down since the *current government* assumed office. 

## Border Tensions and Sovereign Security

Addressing recent geopolitical tensions and human rights violations at the frontier, Azad strongly criticized the current government's diplomatic stance. He alleged that the killings, abductions, and arbitrary detention of Bangladeshi farmers along the border by neighboring forces have continued unabated, without an effective or robust state response to protect its citizens.

---

## The Road Ahead: 36-Day Memorial and Nationwide Protests

To amplify their demands and keep the spirit of the revolution alive, the top leaders of the 11-party alliance held a closed-door meeting last night to intensify their movement. They have drafted a comprehensive roadmap for the coming weeks:

1.  **Nationwide Protests (July 4, 2026):** Protest rallies and processions will be held simultaneously in all districts across the country to reiterate the demand for justice regarding the July-August killings.
2.  **36-Day Memorial Program:** To mark the upcoming second anniversary of the July Uprising, the alliance has drafted an extensive 36-day commemorative and protest program. The final details of this schedule will be confirmed and officially announced following the alliance's next high-level meeting on **June 25**.

## A Unified Opposition Front

The press briefing highlighted the organizational strength of the post-2024 political opposition. Representatives from all constituent parties of the alliance were present to show a unified front. Participating parties included:
*   National Citizen Party (NCP)
*   Bangladesh Khelafat Majlis
*   Liberal Democratic Party (LDP)
*   Bangladesh Nezame Islam Party
*   Bangladesh Labour Party
*   Jatiya Ganotantrik Party (Jagpa)
*   Amar Bangladesh (AB) Party
*   Bangladesh Development Party (BDP)

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Why is the rally being held on June 23?**
June 23 is the historical founding anniversary of the Awami League. The Jamaat-led alliance chose this specific date to hold a counter-rally, labeling it a "painful day" in history and demanding justice for crimes committed during the AL's 17-year rule.

**What are the main demands of the 11-party alliance?**
The primary demands include expedited trials for the July-August 2024 massacres, the 2009 Pilkhana tragedy, and enforced disappearances. They are also protesting the rising cost of living, border killings, and the deterioration of law and order.

**Who will be the chief guest at the Dhaka rally?**
Jamaat-e-Islami Ameer and current Opposition Leader Shafiqur Rahman will attend as the chief guest at the Bijoynagar rally at 5:00 PM.

**What is the 36-day program mentioned in the briefing?**
The alliance has drafted a 36-day continuous political program to commemorate the second anniversary of the historic July Uprising of 2024. The final details will be announced on June 25.
