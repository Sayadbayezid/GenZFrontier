Cody Gakpo Makes Instant World Cup Impact as Liverpool Forward Sends Message to Andoni Iraola

GenZ Frontier Sports Desk | June 21, 2026

Liverpool forward Cody Gakpo helped the Netherlands take a commanding lead against Sweden at the World Cup, delivering a strong performance that served as a timely reminder of exactly what he can offer on the biggest stage.

Setting the tone in Houston, Texas, the dynamic winger played a pivotal role in getting the Dutch side off the mark, teeing up Sunderland striker Bryan Brobbey to open the scoring early in the first half.

A Stellar Performance in Houston

When the Liverpool man was set free down the left flank, he delivered a mathematically perfect low ball for Brobbey to poke home. The Netherlands, who also boast Liverpool stars Virgil van Dijk and Ryan Gravenberch in their ranks, quickly went two goals up in the first half after Brobbey netted a second from another low cross, this time from the right side.

Gakpo, who found the back of the net himself early in the second half, looked incredibly sharp throughout the entire game. His dominant display offered a loud and clear reminder of his offensive talents to Liverpool's newly appointed head coach, Andoni Iraola.

Rising to the Challenge: The Iraola Era

This week, Liverpool completed the signing of Victor Munoz from Osasuna for a fee of $46 million (£35 million). The Spaniard primarily operates off the left wing, meaning he and Gakpo are poised to be in direct competition for crucial starting minutes next term.

However, Gakpo has a proven track record of elevating his game when faced with positional pressure. He demonstrated this exact resilience the season before last, showing massive improvements when he had to aggressively fight for his spot alongside Luis Diaz.

Transfer Rumors and Attacking Depth

Gakpo’s future at Anfield has been the subject of recent speculation, with Tottenham Hotspur reportedly expressing keen interest in a summer move. Despite the noise, the Reds are in a position where they need to bolster their attacking numbers, not deplete them.

While 17-year-old Rio Ngumoha represents another exciting option, his development will require careful, measured management. Furthermore, with Hugo Ekitike sidelined due to injury, Gakpo's versatility is a massive asset. He is more than capable of playing as a number nine, where he could seamlessly help carry the attacking load alongside Alexander Isak.

Reflecting on Arne Slot's Departure

The transition period at Anfield has not been without its emotional hurdles. Speaking a couple of weeks ago regarding the abrupt firing of former manager Arne Slot, Gakpo offered a candid perspective:

"It is, of course, a shame that the manager has been sacked. Especially so soon after the season where we had fought to qualify for the Champions League.

But yes, I believe the club released a statement about that, with the explanation in it. Ultimately, I don’t think anyone, any of the players, saw it coming. But it certainly happened."

With the World Cup demanding total focus, the Dutch international emphasized the need to compartmentalize club drama:

"Many of our players at the club are also working towards the World Cup now, so we’ll have to quickly put that aside, no matter how difficult that can be for someone."

Asked whether he had maintained contact with Slot following the dismissal, Gakpo confirmed he had reached out: "I sent him a message to thank him for the past two years, and of course with the highlight of last season."

What's Next for the Oranje?

Having fought to a hard-earned 2-2 draw with Japan in their World Cup Group Stage opener, the Netherlands are now looking to build momentum. Following their clash with Sweden, they will turn their attention to their next crucial fixture against Tunisia.

Once his international duties and subsequent holiday conclude, Gakpo will return to Merseyside to begin work under Iraola, ready to stake his claim ahead of a highly anticipated Premier League season.
